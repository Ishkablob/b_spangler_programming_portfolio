Weather Hazards TryIt Page

Weather Hazards takes an input latitude and longitude and returns an estimate of how much danger you are in from weather hazards.

A score of 0 means no hazards are nearby. A score of 0-10 means be careful going outside, a score of 11+ means stay inside.

Hazard score is determined off of wind speed, temperature, visibility, and other general weather hazards nearby.

If any of these measures enter dangerous values - i.e. extremely high temperatures, low visibility - the score index goes up.

This service works well with the geolocator service for finding your coordinates from your address.

Try using the geolocator service to find an address's coordinates, then use those coordinates in this service to find a weather hazard index.
